Decentralized E-Voting

E-voting systems are beneficial to all people who are involved in elections.For eg. administrators have better control, and voters can vote in an election anytime and anywhere.In addition, ideal e-voting systems have transparency, completeness (only voters have the right to vote and their votes are correctly counted), and verifiability (voters can check that their vote is correctly counted), and therefore it is better than existing voting system.Security of digital voting is always the biggest concern when considering to implement a digitalvoting system, because of such monumental decisions at stake.One way the security issues can be potentially solved is through the technology of blockchains and decentralization it is a form of distributed immutable database.The blockchain technology is characterized by decentralization, irreversibility, distribution of joint accounting, asymmetric encryption and data-security.

Ballot-privacy (BP)

No outside observer can determine for whom a voter voted.

Coercion-resistance (CR)

A voter cannot interact with a coercer during the election to prove how she is voting.

Individual verifiability (IV)

A voter can verify that the ballot containing his vote in the published set of “all” (as claimed by the system) votes.

Universal verifiability (UV)

Anyone can verify that the result corresponds with the published set of “all” votes.

Blockchain

The public ledger to store the information of signatures and candidate id. It protects the privacy of voters.
